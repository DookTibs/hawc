About us

HAWC development is a collaborative of several individuals and organizations who create, maintain and enhance the functionalities of this tool. The lead developer and conceptual designer of the project is Andy Shapiro who developed the prototype in partial fulfillment of the requirements for the degree of Master of Science in Public Health in the Department of Environmental Sciences and Engineering at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill under direction of Dr. Ivan Rusyn. Overall guidance to the process of development of HAWC is provided by a steering committee with representation from the National Toxicology Program (NTP/NIEHS), Texas A&M University and the National Center for Computational Toxicology (US EPA).
